Ra, God of the Sun

Oh great Ra, we grovel in the sand before thine eternal power! All that is, starts with thou, all that ends, ends with thou. May a thousand tongues sing thine praise for eternity!

Horus, god of Kings

Hail, oh Horus, mighty defender of the Gods! May all praise your valiant struggle against Set, the lord of the Sands. Horus, god of kings, Pharaoh of the sky!

Osiris, king of the Dead

Osiris, king of the Dead, Pharaoh of Pharaohs! All praise be upon thee, as thou sits on thine throne in the endless netherworlds. May thine power forever guard us!

Isis, queen of the Two Lands

Isis, goddess of the living, queen of the Nile! May thine riches forever bring great prosperity on the Black Sands of Egypt. Hail and lo, hail and lo!

Taweret, the mother Goddess

Tawaret, mother of all that lives, breathes and moves, smiles upon the two lands. All hail and respect our mother hippopotamus. May her bountiful waters ever flow on!

Toth, scribe of the Gods

Toth, keeper of wisdom, scribe of the gods. Your mystic hieroglyphs hold many unearthly secrets. May your feathers write on many papyri, oh ibis god!
